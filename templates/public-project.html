About this project

Urban trees and green spaces are increasingly recognised as essential infrastructure for adapting cities to climate change. This project investigates how green infrastructure can be planned and managed to maximise climate adaptation benefits across Flemish cities.

The project combines field measurements, remote sensing data and urban climate modelling to quantify the cooling, flood mitigation and biodiversity co-benefits of diverse urban green infrastructure typologies. Outcomes include evidence-based planning guidelines and open datasets for policymakers and urban designers.
